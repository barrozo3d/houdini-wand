## Structured Notes

### Core Technique
Historical and conceptual survey of three major Houdini systems — FLIP/ocean/hydrodynamics, L-systems, and noise tools — plus a history of Houdini as a software, providing foundational context for understanding why these systems work the way they do.

### Summary
Kate Zegarace (VFX artist at MPC Toronto and Houdini.School teacher) delivers a four-lecture series on the history and evolution of Houdini's key systems. Lecture 1 covers FLIP ocean tools and particle-based hydrodynamics. Lecture 2 explores L-systems and their mathematical basis for generating natural patterns like trees and lightning. Lecture 3 traces the history of Houdini's noise tools from early implementations to the current rich library. Lecture 4 is a history of Houdini itself from PRISMS through to the modern day, including speculation on the future direction of the software. The course is designed to give artists foundational understanding rather than hands-on techniques.

### Key Steps
1. Study the evolution of FLIP fluid simulations and ocean tools in Houdini (Lecture 1)
2. Understand how particle-based hydrodynamics developed over Houdini versions
3. Learn the mathematical basis of L-systems and their application to natural patterns (Lecture 2)
4. Explore the history of Houdini's noise types — Perlin, Worley, Alligator, etc. (Lecture 3)
5. Trace Houdini's evolution from PRISMS to modern Houdini (Lecture 4)
6. Understand the major software milestones — introduction of VEX, DOPs, KineFX, Solaris
7. Consider the future direction of Houdini development

### Houdini Nodes / VEX / Settings
- FLIP Fluid solver
