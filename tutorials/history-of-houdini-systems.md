**Transcript:** Hi, my name is Kate Zeggerarce. I am a teacher at Houdini School, as well as a VFX artist in Toronto, Canada. History of Houdini Systems is a series of lectures focusing on the history and evolution of three different Houdini systems, with the last lecture focusing on the software itself. The first lecture focuses on flip ocean tools, as well as other particle systems that are used in hydrodynamics. The second lecture is about L-systems. L-systems generate natural crack-belt patterns, such as trees and lightning. The third lecture goes over the history of the noise tools in Houdini, as well as the different types of noise in the software. The final lecture discusses the history of Houdini. We will talk about prisms, all the changes to the software up into the present day, and what Houdini might look like in the future. The goal of this class is to give a foundational understanding of how these different systems and Houdini itself need to be. To learn more, visit my class history of Houdini Systems at Houdini School.
