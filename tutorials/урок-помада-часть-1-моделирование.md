## Structured Notes

### Core Technique
Product visualization — lipstick modeling: front-view profile curve → `revolve` SOP (8–12 divisions) → `subdivide` → `fuse` (clean top verts) → angled clip via `box` boolean (5–6° tilt) → `trace` SOP to project brand logo from texture image onto surface. Russian-language, Part 1 of series.

### Summary
A 15-minute Russian-language modeling tutorial (Part 1 of Lipstick series) by Alexander Eskin. Draws a lipstick profile curve in front view, revolves it (8–12 divisions, not critical) with `revolve` SOP, smooths with `subdivide`, cleans top with `fuse`. The characteristic angled top cut is made by clipping with a rotated `box` (boolean, ~5–6°). A logo is projected onto the surface using `trace` SOP from a prepared texture file. Part 2 covers rendering/materials.

### Key Steps
1. Draw lipstick profile curve in front view (beveled oval cross-section)
2. `revolve` SOP — 8 to 12 divisions around Y axis
3. `subdivide` SOP — smooth the geometry
4. `fuse` SOP — weld/clean top vertex pinch point
5. Create `box` SOP → `transform` — rotate ~5–6° to create the angled cutting plane
6. `boolean` SOP — subtract the rotated box to create lipstick's characteristic angled top cut
7. `trace` SOP — load logo image from "textures" folder; trace outline onto surface for embossed logo
8. Continue to Part 2 for materials and rendering

### Houdini Nodes / VEX / Settings

- `boolean` SOP — subtract to create angled top cut
